Your AI project is in trouble. Fix it, or start over?

You are six months in. Something is not working — the outputs are not good enough, the team is stuck, adoption has stalled, or the original plan no longer makes sense. You need an honest answer about what to do next, and you need it before you spend more time and money in the wrong direction.

This is more common than it looks

AI projects fail in predictable ways. Sometimes the technical approach was wrong from the start — the wrong model, the wrong architecture, prompts that worked in testing and broke in production. Sometimes the problem was organisational — unclear ownership, misaligned expectations between IT and the business, no adoption plan. Sometimes the original use case turned out not to be worth solving.

None of these are unusual. Most organisations navigating enterprise AI for the first time encounter at least one of them. The question is not what went wrong — it is whether fixing it is worth the effort, or whether a different approach would get you where you are trying to go faster.

What we do

We run a structured diagnostic that examines your project across four dimensions:

  • Technical — model selection, prompt design, evaluation methodology, production behaviour, hallucination and failure modes
  • Architectural — system design, data pipelines, retrieval strategy, context management, integration points
  • Organisational — team ownership, business and IT alignment, user adoption, change management, governance
  • Strategic — use case validity, success criteria, ROI measurability, fit with organisational maturity

We talk to your team, review the work, examine the outputs, and form an independent view. We are not looking to validate decisions that have already been made. We are looking for the truth about what is happening and why.

What you get

  • Written diagnostic report — a detailed analysis of what is working, what is not, and the root causes behind both — written to be shared with your team and your leadership
  • Presentation with a plan of action — a clear verdict (fix it or reboot) with the reasoning made explicit, the recommended next steps, and what each path requires in terms of time and resource

Where this leads

The plan of action points directly into the next engagement. The verdict determines where you re-enter the journey:

If we recommend reboot

Pilot

A fresh start — different use case, different approach, built on what we learned from the diagnostic.
If we recommend fix it

Accelerate

The foundation is sound. We help you remediate the specific issues and get delivery back on track.
If the build is done

Scale

The technology works but governance, monitoring, or operations are missing. We fill those gaps.

We can run the follow-on engagement ourselves, or hand off the plan to your team or another partner. The goal of First Aid is to give you clarity — not to create a dependency on us.
